Cas12a Off-Target Recognition

Mar. 2025 – July 2025

Cas12b Molecular Dynamics with Split sgRNA

Feb. 2025 – Apr. 2025 · Nature Communications
Performed 100 ns molecular dynamics simulations of Cas12b with various split sgRNA configurations using GROMACS. Analyzed structural stability and binding affinity through RMSD, RMSF, hydrogen bond, and MMPBSA analyses. The Cas12b + sgRNA complex exhibited the highest structural stability and binding affinity among all tested configurations.

G4-crRNA may be inactivated, or it may disrupt the normal conformation of Cas12a, resulting in Cas12a losing its cleavage activity. However, after mutating one of the G4 bases, G4 could not form a normal structure, Cas12a's cleavage activity resumed.

Expanding Cas12a Activity Control with an RNA G-quadruplex at the 5' End of CRISPR RNA: Molecular Mechanism Exploration

Sept. 2024 · Advanced Science
Compared with ① and ②, Cas12a and DR-3′PO₄ in ③ show no visible overall structural changes, but the position of DR-3′PO₄ in the functional region of Cas12a undergoes great changes. To further explain the molecular mechanism, the electrostatic interaction and hydrogen bonding between 22U-PO₄ and neighboring atoms were analyzed. DR-3′PO₄ acts in the positive charge dense region of Cas12a. 22U-PO₄ forms multiple hydrogen bonds with ARG-691 and ASN-590 of Cas12a.
